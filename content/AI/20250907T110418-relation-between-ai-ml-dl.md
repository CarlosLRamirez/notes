## Key Concepts

- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):**
  - It's a broadest concept encompassing machines or systems designed to simulate or mimic human intelligence.
  - Under **AI** umbrella we can include different approaches or sub-fields:
    - **Rule based systems:** Explicitly programmed "if-then" rules (e.g. "if this to that).
    - **Expert systems:** Codified human expertise into logical rule.
    - **Search algorithms:** Chess programs evaluates possible moves.
    - **Machine Learning:** Learning from data (which is just one approach within AI)

> [!TIP]
> Key point: You can create AI without any learning - just good programming and logic.

---

- **Machine Learning (ML):**
  - It's a subset of AI that focuses on systems that can "learn" from data and make predictions or decisions based on that data.
  - Instead of programming explicit rules, you train a model using data.
  - Once trained, the _model_ can predict or make decisions for new, unseen data.
  - No data = no learning = no ML

---

- **Deep Learning (DL):**
  - It's an advanced form of _Machine Learning_ that uses multiple layers of [[20250907T111932-neural-networks|Neural Networks]] for processing data.
  - DL requires large amounts of data for training, often millions of examples to work well.
  - It also requires significant computing power to train many layers of neural networks, for this purpose GPUs, works better than CPUs.
  - DL is better for complex tasks like image recognition, language understanding, and so on.

---

## Other Concepts

- **Generative AI (Gen AI):**
  - AI that generates content, such as text or images
  - Today, when people mention AI, they often refer to generative AI, such as chatbots like ChatGPT or Gemini
  - Gen AI could be classified inside Deep Learning.
- **Data Science**:
  - It's a disciplines that intersects AI and ML.
  - The _Data Scientist_ use statistical and mathematical methods, ML techniques and domain expertise to extract insights from data.
  - The _Data Science_ might use AI/ML tools, but their goal is to analyze, understand and make decisions with the data, rather than build AI systems.
- **Computer Vision and Natural Language Processing**:
  - [[20250907T113120-computer-vision|Computer Vision]] and [[20250907T114034-natural-language-processing|Natural Language Processing]] are applications domains within AI.
  - They're specific problem areas where AI techniques (including ML and Deep Learning) are applied.

## Summary

- AI is the broader concept that covers all systems that mimic human intelligence.
- ML is a way to obtain AI by training models.
- DL is a sophisticated form of ML that uses Neural Networks.
- If AI generates its own content, it's called GenAI.
- Computer Vision and NLP are applications within AI that can use ML/DL.
- Data Science is a discipline that can use ML or DL as tools to work with data.
